INTERNET ACROSS THE WORLD

The internet was invented in 1983 and is almost everywhere we look. This visualisation utilises data primarily from 2018 and aims to show how much people use the internet in modern times.

Internet population usage percent by country

This map shows the amount of people with access to the internet in a country divided by the total population. This gives the internet usage percent shown when hovering over a country.

The internet is no doubt a global phenomenon as it allows anyone with access to almost magically interconnect with systems from all over the world. Giving people access to all sorts of sites and services. It truly is becoming an integral part of human life and will continue to develop further.

Use the slider to filter out certain internet use percentages

Total population and internet users

The internet only became available to the public on April 30, 1993. By 2018 almost half of the population has access to the internet.

The pie chart on the left puts into perspective just how big the proportion of internet users are, considering that it has only been around for not even 30 years.

The internet has almost become a necessity in many people's lives and is growing at a faster rate than the human population.

Internet speeds of 10 Richest vs. Poorest countries

The top and bottom 10 countries were determined by GDP per capita and the internet speed is measured by Mbps (Megabits per second). The red line shows the average internet speed of each set of countries.

Click and drag to see the average internet speed of certain countries or hover over one to see the specific data.

Number of internet subscriptions per 100 people

This chart shows the amount of broadband or internet subscriptions of countries over time. The growth rapidly develops as time goes on and will continue to do so.

Some popular countries are highlighted but feel free to use the drop down box to filter a specific country.
